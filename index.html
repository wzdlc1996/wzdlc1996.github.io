In general, the field can contain some internal degrees of freedom. The Lagrange function now is a functional of the field and its differential respects to time: $$L(t) = L[\phi(\bm{x},t),\partial_t\phi(\bm{x},t)]$$ In general, it depends on the value of $\phi,\partial_t \phi$ at all points in space. Note that the functional does not depend on the coordinate $\bm{x}$ itself.
